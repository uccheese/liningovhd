03 — Subcontracted work
Sub work bid discipline
When you sub out work, revenue includes the sub's cost but gross margin is much thinner. The rules below protect the model.
The core risk
If you apply your self-perform overhead rate to subbed revenue, you'll be over-allocating phantom margin. Your fixed overhead doesn't shrink because a sub is doing the field work — but your gross margin on that revenue is 8–15%, not 35–50%. The difference has to be built into your bid, not assumed.
Self-perform CIPP, own C&TV, own MH
Full overhead rate applies — deploying your equipment, crews, and PM time.
13–15% OH
Subcontracted field work
Reduced OH rate on sub revenue — mostly PM oversight and admin. But the 10–12% markup must be in the bid before submission.
5–8% OH + 10–12% markup in bid
CCTV (own crews) — C&TV
Moderate overhead. Lower materials intensity than CIPP but scheduling-heavy. Megan's video review time is a real cost — Walmart CCTV especially.
10–13% OH
Overhead costs that often get missed on sub work
⚙
Sub qualification & setup
Insurance verification, safety compliance, contract execution. 4–6 hours per new sub relationship. 3–5 subs for the surge = real admin cost against Rusty or Riley's time.
📅
Schedule coordination
Subs don't self-schedule against your project commitments. PM time to coordinate 1 sub on a job = ~10–15% of that job's PM hours. More subs = more coordination overhead.
📄
Invoice review & billing
Joe's controller time increases with sub invoices. Back-charging, pay app reviews, lien waiver management — 1–2 hours per sub per billing cycle.
📹
Video review (Megan)
CCTV footage review is a fixed cost regardless of who ran the camera. If Walmart CCTV materializes, Megan's 50% lining allocation may need to flex — build that into OH contingency.
The rule for Rusty and Riley
Any job where you know you're subbing a portion: sub cost × 1.10–1.12 minimum before bid submission. Don't quote at your self-perform margin rate and then sub it. That's where contractors get crushed.

🔒
10% net profit gate — hard floor for all bonus components
The lining division must achieve a minimum 10% net profit margin before any bonus component is payable — revenue, utilization, safety, or retention. No exceptions. This protects the business from paying bonuses in a year where the division runs unprofitable.
📊
Revenue component also requires minimum gross margin
Beyond the 10% net profit gate, the revenue tier bonus additionally requires ≥ 18% gross margin. Prevents chasing revenue at the expense of profitability — especially critical when subbed work is in the mix.
📅
Annual measurement, paid Q1 of following year
Measured on calendar or fiscal year. Paid within 45 days of year-end financials. Keeps Taylor focused long-term, not month to month.
✅
Must be active employee at time of payout
Standard clause. No pro-ration for partial years unless mutually agreed (e.g., mid-year start date).
📈
Targets reset annually with mutual agreement
Review thresholds each year-end. If division grows to $12M, revenue tiers should scale accordingly. Don't let this become an entitlement.
⚠
Safety override clause
Any fatality or OSHA citation voids safety bonus component regardless of recordable count. This is non-negotiable and should be documented in the comp agreement.
