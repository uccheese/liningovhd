What counts as overhead
Two distinct buckets: (1) Salary overhead — 17% added on top of all direct labor salaries to cover non-billable payroll burden, admin, and indirect labor. (2) Fixed overhead pool — building at $12K/mo, bench time at 1% of revenue, truck/equipment allocation. Subcontract work carries a reduced shared cost since it uses fewer admin resources.

02 — Subcontract Policy
Subcontract Workflow
& Policy
Standard operating procedure for all subcontracted work — from initial contract through final payment. Non-negotiable policies are in writing below.
Non-negotiable: We do not pay until we get paid
This is a written policy. Payment to subcontractors is conditioned on receipt of payment from the client. No exceptions without written authorization from Rusty or division leadership. Cash flow risk on subcontract work is a known exposure — this policy is the primary mitigation.
Subcontract workflow
#StageOwnerRequirements
1 Sub contract issued Riley / Rusty Signed subcontract with scope, price, schedule, and pay-when-paid clause before any work begins
2 Review process PM + Rusty Bid reviewed for margin (18–20% minimum markup), scope gaps, and risk flags before award. Subs carry more risk — less oversight than self-perform
3 Work in progress PM PM tracks field progress. Any scope changes require written change order before work proceeds
4 Sub billing / invoice received PM + Admin Sub submits invoice. PM verifies work completion against contract scope before approving for payment
5 Client billing PM + Admin Invoice client for sub work at agreed markup. Sub costs are a pass-through plus margin
6 Client payment received Accounting Payment confirmed in QuickBooks. This triggers sub payment eligibility
7 Sub payment released Accounting Pay sub per contract terms — only after client payment confirmed. Pay-when-paid is enforced

Accounting bucket question
Should subcontract work be kept as a separate accounting bucket? Recommendation: Yes — keeping sub costs as a distinct line preserves visibility into margin, makes cash flow tracking cleaner, and simplifies the pay-when-paid reconciliation. Less "shared cost" is allocated to sub work since it doesn't consume the same admin overhead as self-perform jobs.
03 — Taylor's Comp
Bonus Structure
& Margin Floor
Taylor's net margin floor is 15% minimum, tracked quarterly. Bonus is layered on top of base. Use the calculator to model payout scenarios.
15% net margin floor — non-negotiable
The division must hit 15% net margin for Taylor's bonus to be active. This gate protects against chasing revenue at the expense of profitability. Below 15%, the bonus pool is suspended — not reduced, suspended. Tracked every quarter, not just year-end.
